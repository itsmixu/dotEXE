# Game Design Document
_This was a part of the jam's submission process, I figured I could put it here for anyone to see if they're interested :D_


## Short Lore Summary
You go up to your computer and it glitches out as you login. You are suddenly in a matrix-like 3D world and as you try to meet the ones who invited you there, problems arise..

### Inspiration
My first idea for this type of game came actually from [Acerola’s video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gg40RWiaHRY) on an ASCII-shader he made. The video sparked an idea in my head of a game that lives in the computer terminal and has similar themes to the Matrix. But the idea stayed until I saw this jam and thought it would be a perfect time to make that game!


## Production
I started making the game on the 22nd, with 9 days left of the jam. I started late, since I didn’t know about the jam until the 21st. 😅

### Development software:
Unity as the Game Engine

Canva for textures/other simple sprites

Bfxr & Bosca Ceoil for sound effects 

Audacity for editing audio clips

### Platform
The game will be made for WebGL so it can be easily played in the browser. A PC build is planned for a post-jam version with several improvements.


## Game Concept
### Theme Implementation
The theme of “Shadows and Alchemy” seemed quite daunting to me at first. As I read the description it is said that shadows can be implemented not just literally as shadows, think shadow governments or secret societies etc. Also alchemy was said to mean different things like transformation, synthesis and hidden knowledge.
The game’s theme revolves around secrets, the unknown and mysteries since the in-game world is set in a matrix-like world where only certain people are let in.

## Visual Style & Graphics
The game was originally meant to use an ASCII-shader to show the game being run in a computer terminal. Long story short I couldn’t use the shader due to restrictions with WebGL. Instead I opted for a pixelated + CRT shader combination and I found it works very well.

## Player Mechanics:
The player has satisfying movement with movement in all directions and a jump. They can also shoot lasers with left click and dash with right click.

## Enemies:
Canonically the enemies are computer programs that try to stop you since you are an unauthorized user there. The enemies would look simple, but still visually interesting. They will shoot lasers, to keep the game simple.

## Audio
The game will have simple 8-bit-style audio effects made with Bfxr. They will fit the game’s theme of canonically being a computer program.
Additional audio clips will be found online and provided with credit of course.

## Post-Jam
I’ve considered making .dotEXE into a full game, I hope to receive feedback on the game so if I decide to develop it further I have a good base to start with. I also have an extensive Trello list with ideas and improvements I want to make in the game’s possible full version. If you have thoughts about the game, please leave a comment! Whether positive or negative, all feedback will be appreciated and helpful <3
